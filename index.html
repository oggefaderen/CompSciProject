What does power feel like in Westeros?

We mapped 3,690 characters from A Wiki of Ice and Fire, then layered relationship scores onto the most connected figures to see who links the story together, and who seems to move through it with warmth, suspicion, loyalty, or contempt.

The Dataset

The project starts with character pages, biographies, affiliations, and quote records. The cleaned second pass removes inflated family-tree links so the network is driven by narrative mentions and affiliations rather than genealogy diagrams.

Why this network matters

A character's importance is not only about page length or fame. In this project, importance comes from the web of named relationships around them. The hubs are the people whose pages point outward to many other characters.

The Story as a Social Map

Each dot is a major character. Lines connect high-degree characters where the cleaned data has a relationship. Color hints at allegiance, while line tone hints at the karma score where one exists.

A Karma Layer

For the top 100 connected characters, a local Gemma model scored how each source character appears to feel toward each target on a 1 to 10 scale. The result is not canon truth; it is a machine-read emotional layer that can be challenged, explored, and improved.
